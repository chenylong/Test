Creating a Simple Multi-series Step Line Chart

In this section, we will create a simple Step Line chart to analyze the change in price of two fictional products over a given period of time. Here, the chart will show you the extent of change in price of both the products at different year thus leading to effective visualization of the variation of price over the same time scale.

Our final chart will look like:

The data for the chart above can be listed as under:

As you can see above, the XML structure for a Step Line chart is similar to that of any Multi-series chart of FusionCharts Suite XT. However, if you are still unaware of the FusionCharts XML structure, let us glance through what we have done.

Here, we have:

  • Created the <chart> element, which is the root element of any chart.
  • Specified caption, y-axis name and number prefix and also hide the data values on the chart. You can specify a lot more properties for the <chart> element, which have been discussed in the Chart XML Sheet.
  • Represented a label in the x-axis using the <category> element within <categories> element.
  • Two <dataset> elements (Product A & Product B) which contain the data of each series.
  • Provided data for both the series in which is to be plotted on the chart in the <set> element.
